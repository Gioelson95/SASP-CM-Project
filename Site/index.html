Project Focus

Our project aims at control different guitar effects like Delay, Chorus and Wah-Wah using the movement of the musicianâ€™s body, tracked by a Microsoft Kinect and a rotation sensor like the one embedded in modern smartphones. We managed to control effects developed by us with SuperCollider and also others included in the amazing H9 by Eventide, using MIDI messages.



Sofware & Technologies Used

We developed 5 different effects using SuperCollider, starting from the knowledge acquired in Computer Music and SASP courses and analyzing some of the best and most famous guitar effects ever made, like the Cry Baby Wah-Wah and the Ibanez Tube Screamer.

Delay, Chorus, Overdrive and Octaver are controlled with the movement. Microsoft Kinect sends the data about the position of the musician to Processing, which converts them in OSC messages, sent over the net to Super Collider. For example you can control the amount of Gain and Volume of the Overdrive simply moving back and forth. A real Wah-Wah is controlled with the movement of the foot, so we use the rotation sensor of the smartphone to track this movement and then, with the help of EywSensorToOSC, the information are converted in OSC messages and sent directly to SuperCollider.

The signal chain in relatively simple, the guitar is connected straight to the Audio Interface, then the sound is processed by Super Collider and finally sent to Logic Pro X through SoundFlower, set with a simulation of a guitar amplifier.



Possible Developments

In the future, in order to improve the project, we will implement a more detailed skeleton tracking, in addition to a system of movement classification and recognition based on Machine Learning (with the help of Wekinator for example). We will also design new effects.
